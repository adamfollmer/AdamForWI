Volunteer

This campaign is built on respect for your privacy while establishing a strong community network. I don't want to collect your name, number, or email address to put you on a list. I want you to be the trusted hub of your own network.

Your Simple Role: Connect and Listen

The strategy is straightforward and personal. I believe the most powerful message is one spoken and heard person-to-person.

1. Watch the Weekly Video

Each week, I will share a video that breaks down one key challenge facing our country and the implications on working families. The goal is to give you the confidence to talk about these issues with other people.

2. Talk to Nine People

Commit to finding 9 other committed people you already know and trust. Aim to connect with three people in each of these three circles:

  • Work/School
  • Friends/Family
  • Neighbors
3. Listen and Share

Listen for ideas that connect back to the videos. Offer a real, authentic alternative to the squeeze people are feeling—whether it's related to their time, their wallet, or both.

Make the connection real – take it offline.

We are looking for 300 core people across every working-class sector—healthcare, education, laborers, public sector workers, and more—to be the direct link to this campaign.

These 300 are the first hubs. As ideas bubble up from your small, trusted groups, the 300 are the people who will boost those ideas and concerns directly to the front of the campaign messaging.

This process is how we:

  • Ensure the campaign stays grounded in real-world experience.
  • Give everyone a voice without collecting mass private data.

Be one of the first 300 or lend a skill

This whole operation is about countering current campaign methods. The website is simple, the videos are amateur, and I'm not a professional in any of these areas. This is the cost of campaigning differently.

If you are a working professional in WI-01 and want to be one of the 300 core voices—or if you have a special skill (like video editing, graphic design, or researching) and want to lend a hand:

Email me directly at adam@adamforwi.com

As we grow, we will establish clear ways for anyone with an idea to contribute, whether it's researching the weekly videos, brainstorming solutions for new policies, or any other way you want to help. We will take the help wherever we can get it.
