Meet Adam

Candidate for Wisconsin's First Congressional District.

Picture

My name is Adam Follmer. I was born and raised in Wisconsin and have proudly called District 1 home since 2016, when I put down roots in St. Francis. I teach at a local university, preparing future speech-language pathologists for a field I’ve spent years working in myself.

During my time working at healthcare facilities from South Milwaukee to Racine to Kenosha, I saw the same story repeated: incredible people keeping the system afloat despite chronic understaffing, heavy workloads, and profit-driven decision-making. Too often, medical care was dictated not by patients’ needs, but by what an insurance plan would cover.

The dedication of healthcare workers has always been inspiring, but the strain they face is unsustainable. I felt it myself, and it lead to me stepping away from full-time clinical practice. What I carried home were ideas: that our healthcare system could be so much more than it is today.

Teaching gave me a chance to help future clinicians enter the field with their eyes open. I worked to modernize training—not to discourage anyone, but to prepare students for the realities of working within a for-profit healthcare system, and to equip them to make it better.

As someone who took on significant student loans, I understand the burden new graduates face. Many were willing to shoulder that debt in exchange for the promise of stable, meaningful work. But as cost-cutting in healthcare grows, that promise feels increasingly uncertain.

What keeps me committed to public service is the same thing that first drew me to healthcare: a belief that people deserve dignity, fairness, and opportunity. I’ve seen firsthand the cost of systems that put profit ahead of patients, students, and working families. I’m running to be a voice for the people who keep our communities strong and to build policies that put them, not corporate interests, at the center.
