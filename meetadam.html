Meet Adam

Candidate for Wisconsin's First Congressional District.

 

My name is Adam Follmer. I was born and raised in Wisconsin and have proudly called District 1 home since 2016, when my family and I put down roots in St. Francis. I currently teach at a local university, preparing future speech-language pathologists for a field I’ve spent years working in myself.

 

During my time working at healthcare facilities—from South Milwaukee to Racine to Kenosha—I saw the same story repeating itself. Dedicated people were keeping the system afloat despite chronic understaffing, heavy workloads, and profit-driven decision-making. Too often, medical care was dictated not by patients’ needs, but by what an insurance plan would cover.

 

I did my best to provide care for the people I served despite the limitations of the system. You don't get into healthcare if you're not dedicated to the job, but the strain our healthcare workers face is unsustainable. This was my first deep appreciation of the idea that our healthcare system could be so much more than it is today.

 

When I transitioned to teaching, I saw a chance to help future clinicians enter the field with their eyes open. My goal was to modernize training and prepare students for the realities of working within a for-profit system, equipping them not just to cope, but to demand better.

 

Yet, the uphill climb for this new generation is immense. Rising tuition, out-of-reach housing costs, and the general increase in the cost of living are stunting their growth. As someone who took on significant student loans for my graduate degree, I understand this burden. I once viewed higher education as a debt worth undertaking for the promise of stable, meaningful work. Today, as cost-cutting measures in healthcare increase, that promise feels increasingly uncertain.

 

The problems I faced in my career are the same ones most Americans face. The wealthy continue to accumulate record earnings while the average citizen is economically squeezed at every turn. What keeps me committed to public service is the same belief that first drew me to healthcare: that people deserve dignity, fairness, and opportunity. I’ve seen firsthand the cost of systems that put profit ahead of patients, students, and working families. I’m running to be a voice for the people who keep our communities strong and to build policies that put them, not corporate interests, at the center of our future.
